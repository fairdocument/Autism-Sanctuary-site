Our Mission

Nature’s haven for autism — and a proving ground for the clinicians who will lead.

Autism Sanctuary is a Central Virginia non-profit dedicated to enhancing the lives of individuals with autism and other developmental and intellectual disabilities (DD/ID) through nature-based connection, agricultural work, and respite care on our 85-acre Edgefield property in White Hall. We serve a population that the broader healthcare system too often overlooks.

Our Clinical Fellows are the backbone of that mission. From the Enchanted Forest to the Edgefield gardens, fellows provide the high-frequency, relationship-based, compassionate care that makes our model work — and in doing so, build a foundation for their own clinical careers that no shadowing rotation can replicate.

Why Choose This Fellowship

The Exchange.

You give us a year of disciplined, compassionate service to a vulnerable population. We give you the clinical foundation, the leadership reps, and the credentialed story that elite graduate programs are looking for.

★

Unparalleled Clinical Hours

Direct, documented hours with DD/ID clients across behavioral, medical, and psychosocial contexts — the kind of hours an admissions committee can’t ignore and a shadowing program can’t deliver.

⛑

Hands-On Crisis Management

Trained, supervised, and trusted to lead in real moments — de-escalation, medical events, sensory crises. You will leave with calm under pressure that most pre-clinical students never develop until residency.

✦

A Prestigious Credential

The Autism Sanctuary Fellowship is a portfolio-grade line item: structured curriculum, written evaluations, executive-director letters, and an alumni network actively writing into top medical, PA, and PhD programs.

⌘

Mentorship That Compounds

One-on-one mentorship from clinicians, executive leadership, and senior fellows. Reflective practice modeled on Project Horseshoe Farm: doing, blended with coaching, teaching, and reading.

⌂

Cohort-Based Living

Live and work alongside peers who have already chosen the harder path. The Edgefield cohort becomes a peer group you’ll keep through medical school, residency, and beyond.

↗

An Up-or-Out Path

A finite 1-to-2-year commitment with a deliberate exit. Most fellows graduate to elite programs; the top 10–20% are promoted to Senior Fellow / Lead DSP and help lead the next cohort.

A Year at a Glance

Designed like a teaching hospital. Grounded like a working farm.

The fellowship blends Project Horseshoe Farm’s emphasis on experiential learning, mentorship, reflection, and community engagement with Autism Sanctuary’s clinical staffing architecture for the DD/ID population.

Service

Direct Care

1:1 and small-group support for DD/ID clients across nature programming, agricultural work, and respite.

Clinical

Behavioral Practice

Supervised behavioral support, sensory regulation, and crisis de-escalation under licensed clinical leadership.

Leadership

Team & Cohort

Lead programs alongside fellow peers; senior fellows supervise interns and university volunteers.

Reflection

Curriculum & Mentorship

Weekly readings, case discussions, 1:1 mentorship, and written evaluations on a 30/60/90 cadence.
