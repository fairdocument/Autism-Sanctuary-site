Autism Sanctuary is a rural care farm on Pea Ridge Road where most participants have significant support needs. We offer fun, engaging days in nature alongside licensed services—grounded in evidence-based practices and promising approaches we have built and refined here. Our Western Albemarle neighbors, volunteers, and partners help make the farm feel like more than a program.

Our model

One sanctuary today—deep roots in Western Albemarle.

We operate a single licensed campus where participants spend real time with trails, animals, gardens, and people who care. Days are designed for joy, regulation, and connection, not a clinical waiting room. Behind the scenes we still do the serious work: training, documentation, and quality standards that keep participants safe and respected.

  • High support needs welcome: Most people we serve rely on consistent, skilled staffing; we plan days realistically and celebrate small wins.
  • Evidence + practice here: We use research-backed methods where they fit, and promising practices developed at Autism Sanctuary when they serve participants well.
  • Local first: Learn more about our campus and how we fit into Crozet, White Hall, and the wider Albemarle community.

Residential & Home-Based Supports

⚙

Workplace Assistance & Pre-Vocational Training

Coaching that connects pre-vocational routines on the farm and trails to community roles when that matches a person’s goals—not a one-size path for everyone.

Workforce & learning

Neighbors, students, and staff who love showing up.

We recruit Direct Support Professionals who are steady, playful, and skilled at supporting people with significant needs. University partners sometimes join us for rotations—alongside the Carol Lynn Siemers Fellowship—so tomorrow’s helpers learn what rural IDD supports really look like.
