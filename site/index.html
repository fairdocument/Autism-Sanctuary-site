Our Innovative Model

A partner-ready Hub-and-Spoke architecture built for quality and scale.

Autism Sanctuary is transitioning from a single site into a coordinated national system. Our central hub sets standards, supports clinical governance, manages compliance, and strengthens operations so local leadership can stay focused on care, community, and therapeutic agriculture.

  • National hub: operational support, quality standards, staff development, payer-readiness, and shared policies.
  • Local sanctuaries: community-rooted, nature-based programming tailored to local families and participants.
  • Flagship continuity: each new site is built from tested practices developed in Virginia.

The University Pipeline

Building the future workforce through allied and clinical pathways.

Autism Sanctuary partners with nearby universities to recruit mission-driven students and graduates as Direct Support Professionals. This creates a living training academy that benefits current participants and strengthens the long-term care workforce.

The Carol Lynn Siemers Fellowship is one component of this pipeline, alongside internships, mentorship tracks, and practical field experience for nursing, speech therapy, psychology, social work, pre-med, and related pathways.
