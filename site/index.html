501(c)(3) · Virginia DBHDS-Licensed · Care Farming

Cultivating independence through Green Care and person-centered community.

We expand a nationally informed, Virginia-rooted model of evidence-based practice—combining licensed lifespan supports with therapeutic horticulture, pre-vocational training, and community engagement for individuals with autism, autistic adults, adults on the spectrum, and self-advocates with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD).

Our Model

A partner-ready hub for standards; local sanctuaries for belonging.

Autism Sanctuary is growing a coordinated network that protects clinical quality while keeping programming human. A central hub strengthens training, compliance, and payer readiness; each sanctuary remains rooted in its community—offering person-centered support through care farming, structured trails, and meaningful community engagement.

  • Interdependence: Participants contribute as trail stewards, gardeners, and peers—not only as recipients of support.
  • Evidence-based practice: DSP supervision, documentation, and training align with IDD service expectations.
  • Flagship learning: Virginia’s campus tests what replication should carry forward. See Flagship Model.

Workforce Pipeline

Building capacity through education partners and the Siemers Fellowship.

We recruit and train Direct Support Professionals who facilitate meaning and motivation every day. University collaborations strengthen nursing, speech-language pathology, psychology, social work, pre-med, and related fields through structured field experience—complementing the Carol Lynn Siemers Fellowship.
