Admissions

Begin with a conversation rooted in respect and clarity.

Our admissions team partners with families, guardians, and case managers—and participants when it fits—to explore supports that blend licensed services with trails, animals, and farm routines. Most people we serve have significant daily needs; we move at the pace of trust and the pace of payer authorization.

Admissions at a Glance

How we partner with you

Step 1: Intake materials

Share information so we can understand strengths, goals, and supports. See Resources for waiver context.

Step 2: Collaborative review

Program leaders evaluate fit, safety, and service alignment using evidence-based standards and Sanctuary-developed practices proven on campus.

Step 3: Person-centered meeting

We meet with participants (when appropriate) and supporters to co-design next steps that honor each person’s dignity and realistic goals.

Step 4: Authorized start

With ISP approval as applicable, we schedule onboarding into the right mix of day, residential, respite, or vocational supports.

FAQ

Questions families often ask

Who can begin the admissions process?

Family members, guardians, case managers, and authorized professionals seeking lifespan support for individuals with autism or IDD may start the conversation. Participants who can self-advocate are welcome to join calls when it helps. We honor communication accommodations.

Do you maintain waitlists?

Some programs may have wait times depending on staffing ratios and licensure capacity. We communicate transparently about anticipated starts.

Are you a nonprofit and licensed provider?

Yes. Autism Sanctuary is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization and a Virginia DBHDS-licensed provider.

What documents help us plan?

When applicable, current ISP, positive behavior support plans, and educational or psychological evaluations from the past two years assist our team in designing safe, strengths-based programming.
