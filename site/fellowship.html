Workforce & Leadership Formation

The Carol Lynn Siemers Fellowship

The fellowship prepares emerging professionals for real-world IDD supports through immersive time on our Western Albemarle farm: trails, animals, gardens, and the pace of rural programming. It complements—but never replaces—our core DSP team.

Learning beside staff who live in boots, not just textbooks

Fellows collaborate with DSPs to support participants—most of whom have significant support needs—across day services, residential contexts when appropriate, and pre-vocational routines on trails and in therapeutic horticulture.

  • Exposure aligned with nursing, SLP, psychology, social work, pre-med, and related pathways
  • Supervision emphasizing documentation, ethics, and respectful boundaries
  • Commitment to neuro-inclusive language and humble listening to families

Real-world grounding

Structured exposure to rural IDD supports with licensed leadership and clear boundaries.

Leadership habits

Communication, coordination, and humility in fast-moving community settings.

Community impact

Contributions that strengthen continuity for participants and families.
