Life as a Fellow

The work is intense. The community is the reason it stays sustainable.

Live alongside a cohort of peers who chose the same hard, meaningful path. Lead programs together. Reflect together. And rest, deliberately, so the work continues to be excellent.

A Week in the Life

Built around the rhythms of Edgefield.

Edgefield is an 85-acre property in White Hall, Virginia — gardens, an apiary, cattle and chicken pastures, an Enchanted Forest of trails and stations. Fellow weeks are structured around the work that happens there, the clients who come to engage with it, and the reflective practice that turns it into clinical learning.

  • ~28 hrs/week direct client care across nature programming, agricultural work, and respite.
  • ~6 hrs/week clinical seminars, case discussions, and 1:1 mentorship with site leadership.
  • ~4 hrs/week operational and program-leadership work — planning, supervision, documentation.
  • ~4 hrs/week reading, reflective journaling, and prep for weekly discussion.
  • 1 full day/week protected MTO (Mandatory Time Off). Non-negotiable.

The schedule extends. Some weeks ask more than others. We are looking for fellows who treat that as part of the deal — and we, in turn, treat your sustainability as part of ours.

Teamwork & Leadership

You will not do this alone — and you will lead before you’re comfortable.

Fellows live in cohort housing in or near White Hall and Charlottesville, commute to Edgefield together, and operate as a true team. This is not a co-working arrangement; it is a clinical unit.

⌂

Cohort Living

Subsidized cohort housing with current and senior fellows. The dinner table becomes one of the most important seminars of the year.

⚑

Program Leadership

By month three, you’re leading a program block. By month seven, you’re mentoring an incoming peer. By month twelve, you’re training your replacement.

⚐

Volunteer & Intern Supervision

Senior fellows supervise university volunteers and clinical interns from UVA and partner institutions — the same supervisory skills required of a chief resident or a research lead.

⌘

Mentorship Triads

Each fellow has a clinical mentor, a peer mentor (a senior fellow), and an alumni mentor — typically a current med student, PA student, or PhD candidate.

✎

Reflective Practice

Weekly written reflection. Monthly recorded case discussions. Quarterly 360° reviews. We treat reflection as a clinical skill, because it is.

✦

Community Engagement

Fellows engage with families, partner clinicians, and community organizations across Charlottesville and Albemarle County — practicing the relational work that sits underneath every functional health system.

Health & Burnout Prevention

If we burn you out, we’ve failed you and your future patients.

DD/ID care is emotionally demanding work. Pretending otherwise is how programs produce cynical, hollowed-out clinicians. Our model treats fellow well-being as a clinical input, not a perk.

Mandatory Time Off (MTO)

One full day per week is protected and culturally enforced. Site leadership covers your shifts. Senior fellows do not text the cohort about work that day. MTO is a discipline, not a suggestion.

Structured Micro-Breaks

15-minute regulated breaks built into every shift, with explicit handoff coverage. Fellows learn what regulation looks like — for clients, and for themselves.

Clinical Debriefs

After any significant behavioral event, the team debriefs together within 24 hours. We name what happened, what we did, and what it cost us emotionally — out loud.

Psychological Safety

Fellows are explicitly trained — and expected — to flag client concerns, clinical mistakes, and personal capacity issues without penalty. The fellowship is a learning environment first.

Confidential Counseling Access

Fellowship-funded access to a licensed clinician outside the chain of supervision — for processing the work, not for performance management.

Physical Resilience

Edgefield is the office: trails, animals, gardens. Fellows are encouraged to use the property for movement, quiet, and the steady restorative work of being outdoors.
