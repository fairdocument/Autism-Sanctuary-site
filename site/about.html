About the Fellowship

Built for clinicians who plan to lead.

A 13-month, cohort-based program with staggered intakes, a deliberate “up-or-out” arc, and a curriculum designed to give you the clinical fluency, leadership reps, and reflective practice that elite graduate programs are looking for.

Section 1 · Structure

The “Up-or-Out” Model.

The Autism Sanctuary Clinical Fellowship is a finite commitment by design. Every fellow joins for an intensive 13-month base term, with the option — by invitation — to extend for a second year of leadership service. After that, the path forward is intentional.

  • ~80% of fellows complete the program and matriculate directly into elite medical, PA, public health, or clinical psychology programs.
  • The top 10–20% are promoted to Senior Fellow / Lead DSP, where they help direct programming, supervise junior fellows, and build the institutional memory of the cohort.
  • No drift, no indefinite tenure. The model exists to develop healthcare leaders, not to retain them indefinitely. Fellows know on day one what the on-ramp and the off-ramp look like.

This clarity is what makes the program rigorous. Fellows are recruited, trained, and held to the standard of clinicians-in-training, not transient staff.

Section 2 · Cohorts

Staggered intakes. Continuous care.

Autism Sanctuary serves clients whose well-being depends on routine, familiar faces, and predictable transitions. Our cohort model is engineered to protect that.

Summer Cohort

July Intake

For graduating seniors, summer-launch fellows arrive in early July and are mentored from day one by the Winter cohort, which by then has six months of institutional knowledge and direct client relationships.

Winter Cohort

January Intake

For mid-year graduates and fall applicants, winter-launch fellows are onboarded by the Summer cohort, which has just hit its peak operating tempo. This rolling handoff is the core of our continuity-of-care model.

Why staggered cohorts matter, clinically.

No bulk transitions.

Clients are never asked to absorb a full staff turnover at once. Familiar fellows always remain on site.

Built-in peer mentors.

Every incoming fellow has a peer-mentor six months ahead of them — the most effective form of onboarding we’ve found.

Leadership reps come fast.

By month seven, every fellow is mentoring an incoming peer. Leadership is not deferred — it’s practiced.

Section 3 · Curriculum & Training

The 30 · 60 · 90 Onboarding Framework.

Modeled after Project Horseshoe Farm’s educational approach — experiential learning blended with reading, reflection, and 1:1 mentorship — and adapted for the clinical realities of DD/ID care.

Days 0 – 30

Foundations & Shadowing

DSP certification, person-centered care training, autism-informed communication, medication administration basics, crisis prevention coursework, and full shadowing of senior fellows on every client routine.

  • Full client roster orientation
  • Daily reflective journaling
  • Weekly 1:1 with site mentor
Days 31 – 60

Supervised Practice

Co-leading routines, beginning independent 1:1 client time under live supervision, joining clinical case discussions, and entering the reading/reflection module on DD/ID care, ethics, and health systems.

  • First written 30-day evaluation
  • Begin “Shadow and Fade” protocol
  • Lead one program block per week
Days 61 – 90

Independent Lead

Fellows lead routines independently, document clinical observations, contribute to interdisciplinary care planning, and begin training in “Consistency Mapping” for the eventual handoff to the next cohort.

  • 90-day clinical competency review
  • First leadership project assigned
  • Mentor an incoming intern or volunteer

Signature Protocol

Shadow & Fade.

Autistic clients deserve more than a polite goodbye and a new face. Shadow & Fade is our structured handoff protocol: across a defined window, the outgoing fellow’s direct involvement tapers while the incoming fellow’s presence rises — always with the client’s routine, sensory environment, and trusted relationships held constant.

  • Begins ~60 days before any planned transition
  • Co-presence sessions logged and signed off by site mentor
  • Client-facing language, prompts, and rituals are explicitly transcribed
  • No “last day” cliff — care simply continues, in different hands
Signature Protocol

Consistency Mapping.

Every client at Autism Sanctuary has a living document we call a Consistency Map — the precise sequence, language, sensory cues, and de-escalation patterns that make their day work. Fellows learn to read these maps, contribute to them, and ultimately author them.

It is the most clinically relevant skill we teach: the discipline of treating a person as a system of preferences, regulators, and triggers — and then handing that understanding off to the next clinician without loss.

  • Authored collaboratively with families and clinical leadership
  • Updated after every meaningful behavioral or medical event
  • Reviewed during every cohort transition
  • Anonymized excerpts feed our curriculum and case-discussion library

Reading & Reflection

Curriculum Modules.

Weekly 2-hour seminar plus assigned reading. Modeled after Horseshoe Farm’s Monday discussion structure, adapted to clinical care for the DD/ID population.

Module I — Foundations of DD/ID Care

History, ethics, dignity-first frameworks, the disability rights movement, and the modern DSP role.

Module II — Behavioral & Crisis Practice

De-escalation, sensory regulation, applied behavioral concepts, and the limits of behavioralism.

Module III — Health Systems & Access

How DD/ID patients navigate Medicaid, primary care, and emergency settings — and where the system fails them.

Module IV — Clinical Communication

Family-facing communication, motivational interviewing, and trauma-informed handoffs.

Module V — Leadership & Operations

Running a cohort, supervising volunteers, managing schedules, and the operational reality of non-profit clinical care.

Module VI — Reflective Practice

Structured journaling, peer case discussions, and 1:1 mentorship designed to convert experience into lasting clinical judgment.
