About Autism Sanctuary

A sanctuary of interdependence for individuals with autism, adults on the spectrum, and self-advocates.

Founded in 2020, Autism Sanctuary is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit and Virginia DBHDS-licensed provider. We enhance lives through person-centered support, evidence-based practice, and Green Care—so participants can grow as trail stewards, gardeners, learners, and community members, not through deficit labels alone.

Our Mission

Nature-connected supports that honor dignity and capacity.

We serve individuals with autism, autistic adults, and people with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD) who benefit from structured, compassionate services. Our mission is to foster belonging, skill-building, and lifespan support through therapeutic agriculture, community engagement, and clinical integrity.

Commitment: We partner with families, self-advocates, payers, and community organizations using clear communication, transparent planning, and respectful identity language.

The Care Farm Model & Philosophy

Social-ecological care: land health and human flourishing together.

Autism Sanctuary operates on a social-ecological model: the well-being of our trails, gardens, and animals is intertwined with the psychosocial well-being of participants. Care farming and therapeutic horticulture are not “extras”—they are structured modalities that create predictable, multisensory environments for learning, regulation, and meaningful contribution.

Green Care routines—designed with clinical and occupational guidance—offer multisensory stimulation (movement, sound, scent, texture) that may support sensory integration goals for some participants. We also design days to reduce passive screen time in favor of active, relational engagement outdoors. Individual responses vary; we do not promise uniform medical outcomes.

Emerging research explores links between movement, nature exposure, routine, and whole-body regulation—including interest in how lifestyle factors may interact with the gut–brain axis in some populations. We describe such concepts cautiously and defer individualized medical claims to a participant’s own care team.
