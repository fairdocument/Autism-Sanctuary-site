Target Audience

Who thrives here.

Recent (or imminent) graduates of four-year institutions, with a clear orientation toward direct service. We have particularly strong partnerships with university volunteer organizations at UVA, William & Mary, Washington & Lee, James Madison, Virginia Tech, and other Mid-Atlantic schools.

⚕

Pre-Medical

Aspiring MD or MD/PhD applicants seeking documented direct clinical hours, longitudinal patient relationships, and a credentialed gap-year experience.

+

Pre-Physician Assistant

PA-bound graduates who want hands-on, supervised clinical reps that PA programs explicitly value over passive shadowing.

ψ

Clinical Psychology & Behavioral Science

Future clinical psychology PhDs, BCBAs, and behavioral researchers seeking deep, structured exposure to the DD/ID population.

What we look for in every applicant.

  • Service track record — sustained, not seasonal.
  • Academic rigor — competitive GPA, science coursework that suggests preparation for graduate-level study.
  • Emotional steadiness — the kind that comes from being tested, not the kind written into a personal statement.
  • Coachability — the willingness to be wrong out loud and corrected in public.
  • Team orientation — fellows live and work together; soloists are a poor fit.
  • Communication — clear, dignified language about people with disabilities.
  • Resilience — fluency with hard days that does not require pretending they are easy.
  • A clear ‘why’ — not “medical school,” but “these patients, this work, this year.”

Interview Process

Three conversations. One purpose.

We are evaluating fit in both directions. Each round is also designed to help you see the program clearly enough to make a real decision.

Round 1

Current Fellow Interview

A 45-minute conversation with a current fellow and a Horseshoe Farm-style alumni representative. Honest, peer-to-peer, no theater.

Round 2

Clinical Leadership Interview

A 60-minute conversation with our clinical lead. Includes scenario-based discussion drawn from real (anonymized) cohort experiences.

Round 3

Executive Director Interview

A final conversation with the Executive Director. Mutual fit, mission alignment, and an explicit conversation about the year ahead.

FAQ

The questions we hear most.

Is this paid?

The fellowship provides cohort housing, a living stipend, and a transportation/educational grant designed to partially offset basic costs of the year. Final figures are confirmed in your offer.

Do I need prior clinical experience?

No. We expect demonstrated service experience and a track record of taking responsibility. Clinical fluency is what we teach.

Can I take the MCAT or GRE during the year?

Yes — and many fellows do. We protect study time within MTO and offer alumni mentorship from fellows who tested mid-fellowship.

What if I want to stay a second year?

Senior Fellow / Lead DSP roles are extended by invitation to roughly the top 10–20% of each cohort. The expectation is leadership of programs and supervision of incoming fellows.
