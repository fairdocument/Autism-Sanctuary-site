After our home campus matures

We’re focused on Albemarle first—future sites come later.

Autism Sanctuary expects to spend the next year or more tightening training, documentation, staffing, and program quality on our Pea Ridge Road campus. We are not opening additional locations during that stretch. This page is for civic leaders, funders, or university partners who want to quietly explore what a future rural care farm might need—not for individual service questions (please use Admissions).

Readiness Criteria

What we evaluate first

  • Workforce ecosystem: Reasonable access to colleges or universities that can feed DSP and allied-health pipelines (a rough guide we used historically was a 20–30 minute commute—your region may differ).
  • Land & farm fit: Parcels that could host therapeutic agriculture, trails, animals, and program buildings with honest attention to accessibility.
  • Community leadership: Partners who understand most participants need significant daily support and still deserve joyful outdoor days.
  • Patience: Licensure, waivers, and ISP rules vary by state; replication only makes sense once our Virginia systems prove they can travel.
