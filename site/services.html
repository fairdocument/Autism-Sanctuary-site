Our Services & Programs

Person-centered supports rooted in evidence-based practice and Green Care.

Autism Sanctuary is a Virginia DBHDS-licensed provider offering lifespan support for individuals with autism, intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD), and related needs. We combine licensed services with care farming, therapeutic horticulture, and structured community engagement—so autistic adults, adults on the spectrum, self-advocates, and family partners experience dignity, belonging, and meaningful roles in a community of interdependence.

Authorization and eligibility: Service availability, waiver funding, and Individualized Services Plan (ISP) authorization vary by person and payer. Our admissions team confirms fit, documentation, and next steps—please see Resources & waiver guidance or Admissions.
Licensed Service Lines

DBHDS-authorized supports delivered with clinical precision and warmth.

These programs reflect the regulatory frameworks through which many participants access services in Virginia. Language on this page is descriptive; specific service definitions and billing categories are confirmed during intake.

Day & Community

Group Day & Community Coaching

Weekday person-centered support that uses our sanctuary as a natural learning environment: therapeutic horticulture, animal care, sensory-informed routines, and guided community engagement with local partners. Participants build communication, self-regulation, and daily living skills—not through isolated tasks, but through meaningful participation in a working care farm.

  • Evidence-based practice embedded in daily coaching and documentation
  • Small-group and side-by-side modeling with trained Direct Support Professionals (DSPs)
  • Community outings that practice independence and prosocial participation
Residential

Residential & Home-Based Supports

Lifespan support for adults who benefit from stable, individualized residential arrangements—including Sponsored Residential, In-Home Support, and Supported Living pathways where authorized. We emphasize continuity, respectful communication with self-advocates and families, and environments that support regulation and growth.

  • Person-centered planning aligned with ISP goals
  • Trauma-informed, dignity-first approaches to daily living
  • Coordination with clinical partners and community resources
Vocational

Workplace Assistance

Workplace Assistance provides structured coaching for competitive or community employment, including task analysis, workplace navigation, and retention supports. Where appropriate, participants transition skills developed through pre-vocational training on the farm and trails into community-based roles.

  • 1:1 and small-team coaching informed by evidence-based practice
  • Employer partnership and natural supports planning
  • Focus on long-term success, not short-term placement alone
Respite & Restorative Programming

Respite care: restorative breaks for families, structured Green Care for participants.

Respite is a highly supportive service that offers primary caregivers a planned, temporary break while participants receive safe, structured programming under professional supervision. At Autism Sanctuary, respite is not “babysitting outdoors”—it is evidence-informed engagement in therapeutic horticulture, nature-based routines, and sensory-rich activities designed to support regulation and joy.

Participants may explore valued roles—gardener, trail steward, animal care partner—that strengthen identity beyond a purely diagnostic frame. Authorization and service codes depend on individual eligibility; our team clarifies options during admissions.

Note: Respite must be specifically authorized on a participant’s plan. We will confirm service type, hours, and payer requirements before scheduling.

Vocational Training & Therapeutic Horticulture

Trail systems as classrooms for pre-vocational skill and regulation.

Our three constructed trails are integral to care farming and Green Care. Walking and working the trails provides aerobic activity that many participants and families associate with reduced anxiety and improved mood—always as a complement to clinical and therapeutic services, not a substitute for them.

Trail maintenance—such as clearing leaves, managing limbs, and keeping corridors safe—is framed as pre-vocational training: it builds fine and gross motor skills, endurance, task sequencing, teamwork, and attention to safety. Under DSP guidance, these routines mirror workplace expectations while honoring each person’s pace and sensory needs.

  • Therapeutic horticulture in gardens and high-tunnel settings
  • Outdoor routines planned with occupational and clinical input as appropriate
  • Progress tied to ISP goals and person-centered outcomes
Community Engagement

Natural learning environments: small groups and one-to-one coaching.

Small-group community engagement

Participants practice social navigation, shared problem-solving, and reciprocal communication in small cohorts. The farm offers predictable rhythms—opening routines, cooperative projects, closing reflections—that support autistic adults and adults on the spectrum to build confidence in group settings.

One-to-one coaching

When individualized goals require it, DSPs provide one-to-one person-centered support for self-care, communication, emotional regulation, and community participation. Coaching is strengths-based: we identify what works for each participant and generalize those strategies across home, program, and community contexts.

Across both formats, Autism Sanctuary functions as a sanctuary of interdependence—where ecological care, participant growth, and community connection reinforce one another.
