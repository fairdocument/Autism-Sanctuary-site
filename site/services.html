Programs & licensed services

Programs that embrace the outdoors.

Autism Sanctuary is a Virginia DBHDS-licensed farm serving people with autism and intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD), most of whom need substantial daily support. Licensed services show up alongside trails, animals, gardens, and friends. We combine evidence-based practices with promising approaches we have built and refined on this farm.

Authorization and eligibility: Service availability, waiver funding, and Individualized Services Plan (ISP) authorization vary by person and payer. Our admissions team confirms fit, documentation, and next steps—please see Resources & waiver guidance or Admissions.
Licensed Service Lines

DBHDS-authorized supports delivered with skill—and a sense of fun.

These programs reflect the regulatory frameworks through which many people access services in Virginia. Language on this page is descriptive; specific service definitions and billing categories are confirmed during intake.

Day & Community

Group Day & Community Coaching

Weekday person-centered support that uses our sanctuary as a natural learning environment: therapeutic horticulture, animal care, sensory-informed routines, and guided community engagement with local partners. People build communication, self-regulation, and daily living skills—not through isolated tasks, but through meaningful participation in a working care farm.

  • Evidence-based practice embedded in daily coaching and documentation
  • Small-group and side-by-side modeling with trained Direct Support Professionals (DSPs)
  • Community outings that practice real-world skills and joyful participation at each person’s pace
Residential

Residential & Home-Based Supports

Lifespan support for adults who benefit from stable, individualized residential arrangements—including Sponsored Residential, In-Home Support, and Supported Living pathways where authorized. We emphasize continuity, respectful communication with families and guardians, and environments that support regulation and growth.

  • Person-centered planning aligned with ISP goals
  • Trauma-informed, dignity-first approaches to daily living
  • Coordination with medical and community partners when people need extra support
Vocational

Workplace Assistance

Workplace Assistance provides structured support for paid or volunteer community roles when that matches a person’s goals—including task analysis, workplace navigation, and retention supports to manage behavior and ensure success. Skills practiced through pre-vocational routines on the farm and trails can transfer when the fit is right.

  • 1:1 and small-team coaching informed by evidence-based and promising practices
  • Employer partnership and natural supports planning
  • Focus on long-term success, not short-term placement alone
Group Day Programming

Full days outside for people with significant support needs.

Group Day gives people a planned routine with safe, structured programming under professional supervision. At Autism Sanctuary, respite still means animals, trails, gardens, and playful routines—planned with the same care we bring to every licensed service day.

People may explore valued roles—gardener, trail steward, animal care partner—that strengthen identity beyond a purely diagnostic frame. Authorization and service codes depend on individual eligibility; our team clarifies options during admissions.

Note: Respite must be specifically authorized on a participant’s plan. We will confirm service type, hours, and payer requirements before scheduling.
Vocational Training & Therapeutic Horticulture

Trail systems as classrooms for pre-vocational skill and regulation.

Our three constructed trails are part of everyday care farming and nature-connected programming. Walking and working the trails provides aerobic activity that many people and families associate with calmer bodies and brighter moods—always alongside, never instead of, the therapies and medical supports a person already uses.

Trail maintenance—such as clearing leaves, managing limbs, and keeping corridors safe—is framed as pre-vocational training: it builds fine and gross motor skills, endurance, task sequencing, teamwork, and attention to safety. Under DSP guidance, these routines mirror workplace expectations while honoring each person’s pace and sensory needs.

  • Therapeutic horticulture in gardens and high-tunnel settings
  • Outdoor routines planned with occupational therapists and nursing partners when that helps the day succeed
  • Progress tied to ISP goals and person-centered outcomes
Community Engagement

Natural learning environments: small groups and one-to-one coaching.

Small-group community engagement

People practice social navigation, shared problem-solving, and reciprocal communication in small cohorts. The farm offers predictable rhythms—opening routines, cooperative projects, closing reflections—that support autistic adults and adults on the spectrum to build confidence in group settings.

One-to-one coaching

When individualized goals require it, DSPs provide one-to-one person-centered support for self-care, communication, emotional regulation, and community participation. Coaching is strengths-based: we identify what works for each participant and generalize those strategies across home, program, and community contexts.

Across both formats, Autism Sanctuary stays a place where land, animals, neighbors, and people share the same sunny afternoons—supportive, unhurried, and human.

How to Begin

With authorization in place, we onboard people into appropriate programs.
