Virginia Flagship

The blueprint for care farming done with clinical rigor.

Our original sanctuary in Virginia is the living laboratory where Green Care, therapeutic horticulture, and person-centered support meet evidence-based practice. It is where we refine what replication should carry forward—so autistic adults, adults on the spectrum, self-advocates, and peers with IDD experience a community of interdependence, not isolation.

Why the flagship matters

The flagship demonstrates how a working care farm can deliver lifespan support with regulatory integrity: structured trails for pre-vocational training, gardens for therapeutic horticulture, and program spaces for community engagement—all coordinated by trained DSPs and leadership.

  • Operational playbooks tested before national scaling
  • Staff development aligned with DBHDS expectations
  • Universal design and sensory-informed environmental planning
  • Quality assurance that protects participant dignity and safety
What New Communities Inherit

Standards, training, and the soul of the model.

Clinical & DSP curriculum

Frameworks for documentation, supervision, handoffs, and interdisciplinary communication—grounded in person-centered planning.

Care farm operations

Protocols that connect land stewardship with participant goals: safe trail routines, horticulture cycles, and animal care with therapeutic intent.

Community engagement design

Templates for small-group learning and 1:1 coaching in natural settings, with measurable ISP alignment.

Replication readiness

Checklists for licensing, payer conversations, and local partnership development—without losing local identity.
