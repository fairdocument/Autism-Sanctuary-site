Our Blueprint

The Virginia Flagship Model

The original Autism Sanctuary in Virginia is our testing ground for operations, curriculum, universal design standards, and nature-based therapeutic programming.

Why the flagship matters

This campus demonstrates how agricultural environments and evidence-informed supports can deliver stable, dignified outcomes for individuals with DD/ID.

  • Refines operational procedures before replication
  • Develops and validates staff training standards
  • Tests participant-centered environmental design
  • Builds scalable quality assurance practices

Blueprint Components

What new sites inherit from the flagship.

Clinical Curriculum

Training frameworks for direct support roles, interdisciplinary communication, and continuity-of-care routines.

Universal Design Standards

Accessible spaces, sensory-aware navigation, and practical layout guidance for farms and program buildings.

Agricultural Operations Model

Therapeutic and vocational farm workflows aligned with participant goals and local community opportunities.

Operational Playbooks

Staffing, supervision, compliance, and reporting systems that support consistent quality.

From one sanctuary to many.

The Virginia site is not the endpoint. It is the blueprint for future Autism Sanctuary locations nationwide.
