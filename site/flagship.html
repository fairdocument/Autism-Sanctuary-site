Pea Ridge Road · Western Albemarle

Our home campus—where the farm is the program.

This is Autism Sanctuary’s only location today: roughly 85 acres of trails, animals, gardens, and gathering spaces woven into Crozet, White Hall, and the wider Albemarle community. Licensed supports happen alongside mud, sunshine, and neighbors who know participants by name. We pair evidence-based practices with promising routines we test and tweak here—not in the abstract.

Why this place matters

The campus shows how a working farm can still meet every DBHDS expectation: structured trails for movement and pre-vocational routines, gardens for therapeutic horticulture, barns and fields for animal care, and porches for rest and regulation—all staffed by DSPs who like being outside.

  • Days built for people with significant support needs, not a glossy brochure
  • Training that blends regulations with the realities of dirt, weather, and livestock
  • Trails, gardens, and buildings planned for accessibility within a real working farm
  • Quality routines that keep participants safe while still having fun
Before any new location

We are still building the playbook—right here.

DSP training & documentation

Clear expectations for notes, supervision, handoffs, and teamwork—so outdoor days stay safe and coordinated.

Care farm operations

Seasonal rhythms for trails, high tunnels, animals, and equipment—balanced with participant energy and weather.

Community engagement

Small groups, 1:1 coaching, and outings that reflect real Western Albemarle partners—not generic templates.

Future growth (later)

Additional sites depend on disciplined systems here first: licensing, staffing pipelines, and relationships that can’t be rushed.
