Resources & Guidance

Funding, referrals, and getting here—without the jargon storm.

This page summarizes common pathways families and case managers use when exploring supports at Autism Sanctuary. It is informational, not legal advice. Eligibility, authorization, and service definitions are always individualized.

Referral & application pathway

  1. Review programs: Read Programs & licensed services to understand what we offer on the farm and in the community.
  2. Contact admissions: Email info@autismsanctuary.org or call (434) 207-2118. For day-program intake questions, you may also reach olivia@autismsanctuary.org as listed on our public intake materials.
  3. Complete intake: Use the process described on Admissions, including documentation such as ISP, behavior plans, and recent evaluations when applicable.
  4. Plan authorization: Our team coordinates with case managers, Community Services Boards, and other partners to confirm waiver or funding alignment before service start.
Funding Frameworks (Virginia)

Waivers and individualized authorization (Virginia)

Medicaid waivers (overview)

Many participants access services through Virginia’s Medicaid waiver programs. Commonly discussed waivers include Family & Individual Supports (FIS), Building Independence (BI), and Community Living (CL). Each waiver has distinct eligibility rules, budgets, and service definitions.

Your case manager determines whether a specific Autism Sanctuary service can be authorized on an Individualized Services Plan (ISP).

Heads-up: Autism Sanctuary does not coordinate Children’s Services Act (CSA) funding. If CSA is part of your conversation, your case manager can help identify appropriate providers.

Accessibility & Transportation

Inclusive access to the sanctuary and community.

Autism Sanctuary is committed to physical and communicative accessibility within the reality of a working farm. Trails, gardens, and program buildings are checked for safe use; individualized plans may include adaptive equipment, pacing, or alternative activities when the weather or terrain shifts.

Transportation: Participants and families often arrange travel through personal vehicles, funded transportation on an ISP where authorized, or regional public and specialized transit. In the Charlottesville–Albemarle area, many neighbors use JAUNT and other local connectors; schedules, eligibility, and routes change frequently.

Verify before you travel: Confirm current routes, paratransit rules, and pick-up locations directly with your transit provider or case manager. Autism Sanctuary does not guarantee third-party transportation availability.
