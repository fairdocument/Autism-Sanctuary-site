Join Our Community

Careers & volunteers: meaning, motivation, and Green Care.

Autism Sanctuary seeks compassionate professionals and community partners who believe in person-centered support, evidence-based practice, and the power of care farming. Whether you are building a career as a Direct Support Professional (DSP) or contributing time as a community volunteer, you help autistic adults, adults on the spectrum, self-advocates, and peers thrive in a community of interdependence.

Direct Support Professionals

Facilitate purpose in a clinically grounded, nature-rich workplace.

DSPs at Autism Sanctuary provide person-centered support across day, residential, and vocational settings. You will coach participants through therapeutic horticulture, pre-vocational training, and community engagement—always within a supervisory structure that prioritizes safety, documentation, and professional growth.

  • Training in evidence-based approaches to IDD and autism supports
  • Mentorship and reflective practice alongside program leadership
  • Pathways aligned with nursing, OT, social work, education, and related fields

Community Volunteers

Climate ambassadors: keep trails safe for nature play and learning.

Volunteer trail maintenance partners are climate ambassadors for our sanctuary. Clearing debris, managing drainage, and maintaining corridors supports safe access for participants, families, and children who use our trails for nature play and therapeutic programming. This work builds the same stewardship ethic we teach participants: care for land is care for community.

Volunteer roles are non-clinical and complement—never replace—paid person-centered support. We match tasks to capacity, season, and safety requirements.

Sustainable Community Sourcing

In-kind donations that fuel creativity and connection.

We welcome gently used items that expand sensory play, art, and nature-based learning—reducing waste while enriching programs.

Nature & exploration

Gently used binoculars, field guides, outdoor exploration tools, and child-safe nature kits (as approved by program staff).

Creative materials

Craft supplies, adaptive art tools, and high-quality reusable materials for structured group projects.

Magna-Tiles & building play

Magna-Tiles and similar magnetic building sets in good condition support cooperative play and fine motor practice.

How to donate items: Email info@autismsanctuary.org with a short description and photo if possible. We confirm acceptance, drop-off windows, and safety screening before accepting materials.
