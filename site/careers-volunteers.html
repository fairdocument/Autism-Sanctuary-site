Join Our Community

Careers & volunteers: show up for people who love the outdoors.

We hire Direct Support Professionals who are steady, kind, and skilled with significant support needs, and we welcome volunteers who keep trails and gardens safe. Days revolve around animals, mud, and Western Albemarle neighbors—paired with training that blends evidence-based methods and promising routines we refine on campus.

Direct Support Professionals

Be the staff teammate who makes farm days possible.

DSPs provide hands-on support across day, residential, and vocational settings. You will coach participants through therapeutic horticulture, pre-vocational routines, and community outings—with supervision that still leaves room for laughter, music, and spontaneous animal encounters.

  • Training in evidence-based approaches plus Sanctuary-developed practices that fit our land
  • Mentorship and reflective practice alongside program leadership
  • Pathways aligned with nursing, OT, social work, education, and related fields

Community Volunteers

Climate ambassadors: keep trails safe for nature play and learning.

Volunteer trail maintenance partners are climate ambassadors for our sanctuary. Clearing debris, managing drainage, and maintaining corridors supports safe access for participants, families, and kids who use our trails for nature play and programming. The work mirrors what we teach participants: caring for the land is caring for neighbors.

Volunteer roles are informal and complement—never replace—paid DSP staffing. We match tasks to capacity, season, and safety requirements.

Sustainable Community Sourcing

In-kind donations that fuel creativity and connection.

We welcome gently used items that expand sensory play, art, and nature-based learning—reducing waste while enriching programs.

Nature & exploration

Gently used binoculars, field guides, outdoor exploration tools, and child-safe nature kits (as approved by program staff).

Creative materials

Craft supplies, adaptive art tools, and high-quality reusable materials for structured group projects.

Magna-Tiles & building play

Magna-Tiles and similar magnetic building sets in good condition support cooperative play and fine motor practice.

How to donate items: Email info@autismsanctuary.org with a short description and photo if possible. We confirm acceptance, drop-off windows, and safety screening before accepting materials.
